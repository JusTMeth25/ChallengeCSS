Hetch Hetchy Valley

What was once described as “Yosemite’s sister valley,” Hetch Hetchy was an expanse surrounded by granite walls, full of life within its meadows, and picturesque waterfalls. Early visitors, in their accounts of the valley, described it in much the same grandeur as the Yosemite Valley.

Located in the tranquil northwestern corner of Yosemite National Park, Hetch Hetchy Valley is a dramatic glacial canyon often called the "twin" of Yosemite Valley due to its towering granite cliffs and massive waterfalls. However, unlike its more famous counterpart, Hetch Hetchy is now submerged under the waters of the Hetch Hetchy Reservoir, created in the early 20th century to provide water to San Francisco. The valley's transformation from a vibrant ecosystem to a reservoir has sparked ongoing debates about conservation and restoration efforts, making it a poignant symbol of the complex relationship between human needs and environmental preservation.
